# 🧙‍♂️ The Wizard of Oz Trap

Faking software automation with backend human labor ("fake it till you make it") which fails to scale and leads to compounding errors and operational collapse.

---

## ScaleFactor (YC W17)
* **What they built**: A pure B2B SaaS platform pitched as the ultimate "AI-powered accounting software." It promised small business owners that complex machine-learning algorithms would automatically reconcile their books, manage payroll, and generate financial reports without the need for an expensive human accountant.
* **The Failure**: The core software didn't actually work. To keep up the illusion of automated, flawless AI, ScaleFactor heavily relied on a "Wizard of Oz" technique: they secretly hired an offshore team of accountants in the Philippines to manually balance the books on the backend using standard tools like QuickBooks. However, human labor couldn't scale at the speed of their aggressive venture-backed user acquisition. The manual bookkeeping became overwhelmed, error-prone, and delayed. Small businesses suddenly found their financials were a complete disaster, with missing thousands of dollars and ruined tax returns.
* **The Outcome**: The facade ultimately collapsed. Despite raising an astonishing &#36;100 million from top-tier VCs like Bessemer and Coatue, the massive discrepancy between what the software claimed to do and the human labor required to actually do it caught up to them. They were forced to abruptly shut down operations in 2020.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
