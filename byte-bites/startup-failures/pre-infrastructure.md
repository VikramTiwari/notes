# ⏳ Too Early / Pre-Infrastructure

Sometimes the product vision is correct, but the surrounding hardware or internet infrastructure is not mature or cheap enough to support it.

---

## NewsTilt (YC W10)
* **What they built**: A publishing and monetization CMS for independent journalists (essentially Substack in 2010).
* **The Failure**: They built a text editor but missed the core problem: writers in 2010 didn't have an audience. The creator economy and newsletter subscription models weren't yet normalized.
* **The Outcome**: Shut down a few months after their batch.

---

## Nitrous.io (YC S12)
* **What they built**: A cloud-based IDE allowing developers to spin up development environments in the browser.
* **The Failure**: They predicted the future (like GitHub Codespaces), but in 2012 browser latency was too high, cloud hosting was expensive, and developers refused to pay for what they could run locally for free.
* **The Outcome**: Shut down in 2016 and deleted all hosted user code.

---

## Easel (YC W12)
* **What they built**: An in-browser collaborative web design editor (essentially Figma in 2012).
* **The Failure**: Browsers in 2012 lacked the graphic rendering efficiency (like WebGL) to provide designers with a pixel-perfect, lag-free canvas, and developers preferred native desktop apps.
* **The Outcome**: Acqui-hired by GitHub in 2014; the Easel editor was discontinued.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
