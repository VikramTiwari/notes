# 🧳 The SaaS Consolidation Trap

Building a niche internal enterprise tool that is vulnerable to corporate budget cuts and tech stack consolidation onto massive, existing platforms.

---

## Ariglad (YC 2023)
* **What they built**: A pure software B2B SaaS tool specifically designed for HR departments. It used AI to act as a smart "help desk," automatically categorizing, routing, and answering internal employee tickets (like payroll questions or benefits inquiries) via Slack and email.
* **The Failure**: Ariglad built a highly functional product, but they ran directly into the buzzsaw of corporate budget tightening. When the tech market cooled in 2023 and 2024, enterprise companies stopped buying niche software for specific internal departments. IT managers began ruthlessly consolidating their tech stacks. Instead of paying a separate SaaS subscription just for an HR help desk, companies forced their HR teams to simply use the massive enterprise ticketing systems they were already paying for (like Jira, Zendesk, or ServiceNow).
* **The Outcome**: Despite having great AI routing software and raising over &#36;1M, they couldn't overcome the enterprise trend of SaaS consolidation. They recently landed on tech deadpool lists, proving that internal corporate efficiency tools are often the first things cut when CFOs tighten the belt.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
