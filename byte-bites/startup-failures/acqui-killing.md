# 💀 Acqui-killing & Corporate Neutralization

Getting acquired by a giant corporation is often the death sentence for the software product itself, which is neutralized to protect the parent company's ecosystem.

---

## Skiff (YC W20)
* **What they built**: An end-to-end encrypted workspace (Mail, Docs, Drive) serving as a privacy-centric alternative to Google Workspace.
* **The Failure**: Highly expensive to run and difficult to monetize. They were acquired by Notion in 2024.
* **The Outcome**: Notion shut down Skiff's software within six months, using the engineering team to build out Notion's document features, causing public outrage.

---

## Rapportive (YC S10)
* **What they built**: A universally beloved browser extension that displayed social profiles of contacts directly inside Gmail's sidebar.
* **The Failure**: Acquired by LinkedIn in 2012 for &#36;15M. LinkedIn did not want it to operate independently; they stripped away Facebook and Twitter integrations to push sales reps to buy their Sales Navigator tool.
* **The Outcome**: The product became hollowed out, lost its user base, and was retired.

---

## Screenhero (YC W13)
* **What they built**: A remote screen-sharing tool for remote developers featuring independent cursors and ultra-low latency.
* **The Failure**: Acquired by Slack in 2015 for &#36;30M to integrate interactive tools natively into Slack's call system.
* **The Outcome**: Slack killed the standalone Screenhero app. Rebuilding it natively inside Slack was buggy, and Slack eventually deprecated the cursor-sharing features altogether.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
