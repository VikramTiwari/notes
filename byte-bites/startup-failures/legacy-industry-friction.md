# 🏥 Legacy Industry & Bureaucracy Friction

Selling software to slow-moving traditional sectors like healthcare, finance, and agriculture requires navigating complex regulation, long sales cycles, and systemic friction.

---

## Call9 (YC S15)
* **What they built**: A telemedicine platform connecting nursing home staff with remote ER doctors.
* **The Failure**: The US healthcare system was set up for "fee-for-service" (paying for physical ambulances). Insurers and Medicare lacked the administrative mechanisms to pay Call9 for preventing ambulance rides.
* **The Outcome**: Despite raising &#36;34M and proving the tech worked, they ran out of cash and shut down in 2019.

---

## Opkit (YC S21)
* **What they built**: An AI voice assistant designed to automate phone calls between medical clinics and insurance companies.
* **The Failure**: Integrating AI phone agents into outdated, legacy medical workflows carried extreme HIPAA compliance burdens and zero tolerance for hallucinations.
* **The Outcome**: The company went inactive due to extremely slow enterprise sales cycles.

---

## FarmLogs (YC W12)
* **What they built**: A SaaS analytics dashboard helping farmers track crop yields, weather, and market prices.
* **The Failure**: Farmers are highly price-sensitive and operate on long, once-a-year harvest cycles. Selling SaaS to a traditional industry with legacy tractor integrations made venture-scale growth metrics impossible to hit.
* **The Outcome**: Underwent heavy layoffs and was acquired by Bushel in a 2021 fire sale.

---

## Standard Treasury (YC S13)
* **What they built**: An API platform allowing developers to integrate legacy banking systems into modern fintech applications.
* **The Failure**: Bank enterprise sales are incredibly slow. Furthermore, banks wanted to build and control their own APIs rather than rely on a startup middleware layer.
* **The Outcome**: Acqui-hired by Silicon Valley Bank (SVB) in 2015; the Standard Treasury platform was retired.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
