# 🔐 Breaking Developer Trust

Developer tooling requires absolute transparency; attempting to growth-hack developers with hidden tracking will destroy the brand instantly.

---

## Kite (YC S14)
* **What they built**: An AI-powered code completion tool for developers. Long before ChatGPT existed, Kite built machine-learning software that ran locally inside your IDE, analyzing your codebase to predict and write the next lines of code for you.
* **The Failure**: Kite suffered from two fatal software issues. First, timing: running deep-learning models locally in 2014–2018 drained CPU and RAM, making developers' laptops run agonizingly slow. Second, and more importantly, they broke developer trust. In an attempt to force growth, Kite secretly acquired popular open-source plugins and injected their proprietary tracking and software into them without telling the community. The developer backlash was instantaneous and severe.
* **The Outcome**: By the time cloud infrastructure and LLMs became cheap enough to actually make AI coding work flawlessly, GitHub Copilot launched with massive distribution. Kite was already dealing with a burned reputation and completely ran out of money. They officially shut down in 2021, proving that developer trust is the most fragile currency in software.
## Triplebyte (YC S15)
* **What they built**: A highly sophisticated technical hiring platform that had engineers take an intensive, blind coding test to automatically fast-track them to final-round interviews at top tech companies.
* **The Failure**: Triplebyte built a massive network of elite engineers entirely on the premise of privacy. Developers loved the software because they could secretly test their market value without their current employers knowing. However, under immense pressure to hit venture-scale growth and pivot into a "LinkedIn for Engineers," Triplebyte pushed a catastrophic platform update that flipped private developer profiles to "public" by default, exposing engineers' job-seeking statuses to the open web and their current employers.
* **The Outcome**: The developer backlash was nuclear, and engineers deleted their accounts en masse. The platform's entire underlying value—its trusted, active network of top-tier talent—evaporated almost overnight. The company was acquired in a desperate fire sale by Karat in 2023, and the standalone Triplebyte platform was permanently shut down.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaway
For startups in this category, the core challenge is not the code but the surrounding market dynamics. Ensure you validate this bottleneck before scaling.
